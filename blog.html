Short Story: The Dentist Visit

Every day as I walked back home from school, I would always buy myself a lollipop. Growing up in town, licking lollipops was my favourite thing. One night, I had a very serious pain in my tooth. I told my parents about it, and they gave me a painkiller to reduce the pain. Early in the morning, I woke up totally fine, like nothing had happened the previous night. I went to the bathroom, grabbed my toothbrush, and started brushing. Suddenly, I began to feel pain in my tooth. The pain got worse every second, as if brushing was an “increase pain” button. I tried to take the painkillers I had used the previous night, but it seemed to be getting worse. I told my parents about the situation, and they took me to the dentist. “Good morning,” said my mom to the receptionist. “Good morning, ma’am,” replied the receptionist. “How can I help today?” “My son has an appointment with Dr. Mark,” my mom replied. “Well, he’s inside his office upstairs.” “Thanks.” “My pleasure,” said the receptionist. We went upstairs to his office. While he was having a conversation with my mom, I was buried in thoughts about how painful it would be to remove my tooth. After a short conversation with my mom, he said to me, “Follow me, son.” I followed him to the room where he performs extractions. Inside was a large dental chair, and on the table were many tools. Immediately, my heart began to race. As I looked at the tools, I imagined how painful the procedure would be. “Are you scared, son?” said the dentist. “The procedure won’t be painful, because we use a special drug that makes you not feel pain.” I was relieved by what he said. After carefully examining my teeth, he said he had to remove the tooth that was infected. I nodded fearfully. He gave me an injection to avoid the pain. After the removal, he gave me medicine to take whenever I experienced pain. I then went back to my mom in the waiting room. After we went home, I never experienced pain in that tooth again.

Poem: When I Die Young

When I die young, Place my body on a bed of roses. Put me on the flowing river, Let me sail westward, The direction where the sun sets. For my loved ones will remember me By the twilight of the setting sun, Not by the grave I lie beneath.

Essay: Forms of Violence in South Sudan

Every day, people lose their lives due to violence. Violence exists in different forms, from gender-based violence to sexual abuse and others. In this essay, we are going to discuss forms of violence in South Sudan and their possible solutions. Firstly, let us define violence. Violence is an action that causes destruction, pain, or suffering. In South Sudan, people suffer from violence almost every day. One common form of violence is intercommunal violence, which arises from disputes between two or more communities. Moreover, the common causes of intercommunal violence include cultural differences, competition over natural resources, and other related factors. However, it is worth noting that intercommunal violence is not the only form of violence. Sexual abuse, for example, is another major form of violence in South Sudan. One possible solution to sexual abuse is to sensitise communities about the importance of cultural values and personal dignity. People should also be educated about health risks such as sexually transmitted infections (STIs) associated with sexual violence, including rape. In conclusion, it is the responsibility of every individual to promote peace and reduce deaths and injuries caused by violence.
